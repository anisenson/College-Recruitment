Student Life

Student Success Center

The Student Success Center at James Madison University offers a dynamic, supportive environment designed to enhance student learning, well-being, and services. As a central hub, it provides a range of programs and resources to help students improve their academic efficiency, engagement, and effectiveness. The center features spaces for studying, collaboration, and learning, and it offers guidance and support for various needs, including financial, academic, technical, health (both physical and mental), and career-related concerns. Living on Campus

Our students come from both large cities and small towns, representing all corners of the United States and over 50 different countries. At JMU, students quickly feel at home within our welcoming community. This is a place where people hold doors for each other, are enthusiastic about meeting new friends, enjoy grabbing lunch between classes, and are open to exploring new fitness activities. • In-state tuition is approximately $14,242
• Out-of-state tuition is around $31,588
+ an estimated $12,916 for Housing and Food

Graduate Tuition:
For the 2024-2025 academic year
Varies by program-- Per credit hour, most programs

• In-state: $561
• Out-of-state: $1,291.
Average costs range from $15,000 to $25,000 per year.

Financial Aid

Scholarships: JMU offers a variety of merit-based and need-based scholarships, including the Madison Award, which recognizes outstanding students. Additional scholarships are available for specific majors, minority groups, and incoming freshmen.

Loans: Federal Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans are available for eligible students. Private loans may also be an option, but terms and conditions vary widely.

Grants: The Federal Pell Grant is available for undergraduates demonstrating significant financial need. State grants, such as the Virginia Tuition Assistance Grant, are also accessible for in-state students.
